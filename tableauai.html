Gender & Ethnic Disparities in Tech Companies

Overview

Diversity in technology companies remains a pressing issue. While progress has been made, women and underrepresented ethnic groups continue to face challenges in representation, especially in leadership roles.

Key Insight: Women of color are the most underrepresented group in senior positions across major tech firms.

Gender Representation

Men make up over 70% of technical roles. Women are more likely to be found in non-technical positions, and their presence in executive leadership remains below 20%.

Ethnic Representation

White and Asian employees are disproportionately represented in tech, while Black and Hispanic employees remain underrepresented, particularly in management and executive roles.

Moving Forward

Addressing these disparities requires intentional recruitment, mentorship programs, and equitable promotion practices. Companies that prioritize diversity and inclusion benefit from stronger innovation and broader market reach.
