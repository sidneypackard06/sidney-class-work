Grandma’s Classic Apple Pie

A Pioneer Woman–style recipe with love and butter

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter (cold, cut into cubes)
  • 6–8 tbsp ice water
  • 6 medium apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp butter (to dot the filling)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • 1 tbsp milk


Directions

Step 1: Make the crust. In a large bowl, mix flour and salt. Cut in cold butter with a pastry cutter or fork until mixture looks like coarse crumbs. Add ice water one tablespoon at a time until dough comes together. Divide into two discs, wrap in plastic, and chill for 30 minutes.
Step 2: Prepare the apples. Peel, core, and slice apples into thin wedges. Place in a large bowl and toss with sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice until evenly coated.
Step 3: Roll out the bottom crust. On a floured surface, roll one dough disc into a circle about 12 inches wide. Place into a 9-inch pie pan, letting excess hang over the edges.
Step 4: Fill the pie. Pour apple mixture into crust, spreading evenly. Dot with small pieces of butter.
Step 5: Roll out the top crust. Roll the second dough disc into another circle. Place over apples. Trim excess, fold edges under, and crimp with fingers or fork. Cut small slits in the top for steam to escape.
Step 6: Egg wash. Beat egg with milk and brush over the top crust for a golden finish.
Step 7: Bake. Place pie in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C). Bake for 50–60 minutes, until crust is golden brown and filling is bubbling. If edges brown too quickly, cover with foil.
Step 8: Cool and serve. Let pie cool for at least 2 hours before slicing to allow filling to set. Serve with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream.

Tips for Beginners

  • Keep butter cold for a flaky crust.
  • Don’t overwork the dough—gentle hands make tender pastry.
  • Use a mix of tart and sweet apples for balanced flavor.
  • If rolling dough feels tricky, roll between two sheets of parchment paper.
Baked with love — just like Grandma used to make.
